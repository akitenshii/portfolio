Animation

Urgency Animation

I decided to animate urgency through my animation, making it feel fast, stressful, and like the character made of simple shapes is rushing not to be late. I used motion lines, fast movement, and exaggerated actions to show speed, along with visuals like “zzz” symbols and big red alarm clock numbers to show the shift from calm sleep to panic. While moving from storyboard to final animation, I added more movement, effects, transitions, and adjusted timing in After Effects to better show urgency and improve flow, which made the final animation more detailed and emotional than my original plan. I used all four transform properties—position, rotation, scale, and opacity—to create movement, zooms, fades, and smoother motion throughout the scenes. The hardest part was organizing the Illustrator layers and separating what needed to be animated from the backgrounds, but once organized, the animation process became much easier and more efficient.

Software used: Adobe After Effects and Illustrator
